SCENARIO 4 — BGP session debugging
Source: pybatfish notebook "Introduction to BGP Analysis" +
networks/example-bgp

THE NETWORK
The AS1/AS2/AS3 example network, but with several BGP misconfigurations
seeded on purpose (see network-diagram.png). The notebook walks through
debugging sessions that show up as NOT_COMPATIBLE, NOT_ESTABLISHED,
UNKNOWN_REMOTE, LOCAL_IP_UNKNOWN_STATICALLY and NO_LOCAL_IP.

HOW TO RUN
1. Upload every .cfg in configs/, click "Build snapshot".
2. Ask the questions below.

QUESTIONS TO ASK
1) "Show me the BGP session status for the whole network. Which sessions are
    not established, and on which devices?"
   (expect a mix of ESTABLISHED and broken sessions — the summary counts and
    the per-neighbor list come from the engine's bgpSessionStatus)

2) "Why is the BGP session for the peer on as1border2 not compatible? Debug
    it."
   (notebook storyline: an UNKNOWN_REMOTE — the configured neighbor has no
    matching peer in the network)

3) "Debug the not-established session on as2border2 — what is wrong with its
    local IP?"
   (notebook storyline: LOCAL_IP_UNKNOWN_STATICALLY — missing
    update-source/local address)

4) "Overall health check: any parse issues, undefined references, duplicate
    IPs, or forwarding loops in this snapshot?"

WHY THIS SET IS INTERESTING
Session-status facts used to be invisible to this app (the MCP layer hides
bgpSessionStatus); they are now first-class engine facts, and this network
is purpose-built to exercise every broken-session flavor.
